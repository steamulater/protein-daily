---

## What Even Is a Protein?

Let's start there, for the people who clicked this link not entirely sure.

Your body is running right now. Heart beating. Lungs expanding. Brain reading these words. Every single one of those things is happening because of proteins — tiny molecular machines, a few billionths of a meter wide, each one folded into a precise three-dimensional shape that lets it do exactly one job with extraordinary specificity.

There are around 20,000 different proteins in the human body. They build your muscles. They carry your oxygen. They fight your infections. They copy your DNA. They decide whether a cell lives or dies. They are, in a very real sense, the reason you are alive and not just a warm bag of chemicals slowly falling apart.

And yet most people will live their entire lives without hearing the name of a single one.

That ends here.

## Two Audiences, One Series

Here's the thing about molecular biology: it is genuinely fascinating to everyone, and genuinely confusing to almost everyone at the same time.

So I'm writing this for both:

**If you're new to this** — every entry will tell you what the protein does, why it matters to your actual life, and one fact that will stick with you. No textbook required. No prior knowledge assumed. Just curiosity.

**If you're a researcher or protein designer** — every entry will link to the PDB structure, give you the real numbers, and occasionally get into the structural biology that makes these molecules remarkable. You'll probably disagree with some of my takes. Feel free. That's what the comments are for.
